Reverse-Reloaded (the “Add-In”) is published under the brand name phos.systems by the data controller named above.

1. Data the Add-In collects

The Add-In does not collect, transmit, or process any personal data. All operations — mesh selection, surface fitting, BRep construction — are performed locally inside Autodesk Fusion on the user’s own machine. The Add-In contains no telemetry, no analytics, no error reporting, and no usage metrics, and it does not transmit any information off the user’s machine.

Note: Autodesk Fusion itself, the host application in which the Add-In runs, may collect data under Autodesk’s own privacy policy. Any such collection is performed by Autodesk and is independent of, and outside the scope of, this policy.

2. Third-party sharing

Because the Add-In does not collect any data, there is no data to share with third parties. The Add-In does not bundle, embed, or call any third-party analytics SDK, advertising network, or telemetry service, and it has no legal affiliates with whom such data could be shared.

If a future version of the Add-In ever introduces data collection or sharing, this policy will be updated before that version is released, and any third party receiving data will be contractually required to provide the same level of protection for that data as is set out in this policy.

3. Retention and deletion

The Add-In retains no data. Because no personal data ever leaves the user’s machine, there is nothing on the publisher’s side to retain and nothing to delete.

4. Revoking consent and requesting deletion

No consent is collected by the Add-In, because no personal data is processed. If you nevertheless wish to ask a question, raise a concern, or obtain written confirmation of the above, contact the data controller at info@phos.systems.

To remove the Add-In itself from your system, uninstall it from the Add-Ins dialog inside Autodesk Fusion or from the Autodesk App Store.

Your rights under GDPR

Users in the European Union have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (Articles 15–22), including the right of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection. Because the Add-In processes no personal data, these rights have no data to apply to in the context of the Add-In, but users remain free to contact the data controller at the address above with any related question.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in a future release, the updated version will ship with the new release of the Add-In and will be republished at the same public URL. The effective date at the top of this document will reflect the change.
